Review of the genus Cilliba von Heyden (Acari: Uropodina: Cillibidae)

Marcin Stachowiak, Bruce Halliday, Jerzy Bloszyk

Pages: 1-42

Abstract

We review the systematics, biogeography, ecology, and phylogeny of the genus Cilliba von Heyden (Acari: Uropodina: Cillibidae). The genus includes eight species, all from Europe. Keys are provided for identification of the females, males, and deutonymphs. Re-descriptions are given for C. athiasae (Hirschmann & Zirngiebl-Nicol), C. cassidoidea (Hirschmann & Zirngiebl-Nicol), C. erlangensis (Hirschmann & Zirngiebl-Nicol), C. sellnicki (Hirschmann & ZirngieblNicol) and C. stammeri (Hirschmann & Zirngiebl-Nicol). The only other genus in the family Cillibidae is Australocilliba Athias-Binche & B»oszyk, which occurs in Australia and South America. Most species of Cilliba are bisexual and probably arrhenotokous, but C. erlangensis and C. rafalskii B»oszyk et al. appear to be thelytokous.

Checklist of the Decapods (Crustacea) from the Fernando de Noronha Archipelago, Brazil

Maria De Lourdes Alves, Marilena Ramos-Porto, Girlene Fábia Segundo Viana

Pages: 43-68

Abstract

This work presents a list of crustacean decapods species collected around Fernando de Noronha Archipelago, between November 1991 and August 1993, and also January 2000. This report lists others species reported in the area from records in the literature as well as those collected during the Almirante Saldanha Expedition. A total of 133 species are recorded belonging to 45 families from the Suborder Dendrobranchiata and the Infraorders Stenopodidea, Caridea, Astacidea, Thalassinidea, Palinura, Anomura and Brachyura of the Suborder Pleocyemata.

Keywords

Checklist; Crustacea; Decapods; Fernando de Noronha Archipelago
