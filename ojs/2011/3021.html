An illustrated key to fruit flies (Diptera: Tephritidae) from Peninsular India and the Andaman and Nicobar Islands

K. J. David, S. Ramani

Pages: 1-31

Abstract

[en_US] An illustrated key and a checklist are provided for 126 species of fruit flies under 46 genera in four subfamilies namely Dacinae, Phytalmiinae, Tephritinae and Trypetinae. Among these, Acroceratitis striata (Froggatt), Rhochmopterum venustum (de Meijere) and Themara yunnana Zia are new records for India. Bactrocera yercaudiae Drew is placed as a synonymn of Bactrocera digressa Radhakrishnan.

Keywords

Oriental Tephritidae; Dacinae; Phytalmiinae,Tephritinae; Trypetinae; India

Review of the genus Truljalia Gorochov (Orthoptera: Gryllidae; Podoscirtinae; Podoscirtini) from China

Hao-Yu Liu, Fu-Ming Shi

Pages: 32-38

Abstract

[en_US] The genus Truljalia Gorochov from China is reviewed. A new species (China: Yunnan), Truljalia multiprotubera sp. nov. is described. T. prolongata Wang & Woo, 1992 is considered as a junior synonym of T. meloda Gorochov, 1992. T. viminea Gorochov, 2002 is recorded to China for the first time, but T. hofmanni (Saussure) is excluded from Chinese fauna. A distribution map of all Truljalia species is presented.

Keywords

Orthoptera; Gryllidae; Truljalia; new species; new synonym; China

A taxonomic revision of Tridrepana Swinhoe, 1895 in China, with descriptions of three new species (Lepidoptera, Drepanidae)

Wenhui Song, Dayong Xue, Hongxiang Han

Pages: 39-62

Abstract

[en_US] The genus Tridrepana Swinhoe in China is reviewed, nineteen species are recognized, of which, three new species T. subadelpha sp. nov., T. bicuspidata sp. nov. and T. subunispina sp. nov. from Yunnan and Hainan are described; two new synonyms are introduced: T. leva Chu & Wang, 1988. syn. n. (= T. crocea (Leech, 1888)) and T. flava sinica Chu & Wang, 1988. syn. n. (= T. flava flava (Moore, 1879)); T. emina is sunk to be a subspecies of T. arikana; T. argentistriga Warren, 1896 and T. rubromarginata indica Watson, 1957 are recorded in China for the first time. Descriptions for the genus and new species, a key and diagnoses for the Chinese species are provided. Illustrations of the external features and the genitalia are presented.

Keywords

Drepanidae; Tridrepana; taxonomy; revision; new species; new synonymy; China
