Cladistic analysis of Abracrini genera (Orthoptera, Acrididae, Ommatolampinae)

Maria Kátia Matiotti Da Costa, Gervásio Silva Carvalho, Carmem Silvia Fontanetti

Pages: 1-25

Abstract

[en_US] A cladistic analysis using 50 characters and 21 genera of the Neotropical tribe Abracrini was performed in order to test the monophyly of the tribe and to reconstruct a phylogenetic hypothesis. Abracrini is supported by three synapomorphies: penultimate article of maxillary palps unflattened; second segment of hind tarsi short and internal apophysis of cerci vestigial. The analysis demonstrates that Abracrini is a monophyletic tribe, as well as a relationship among its genera was (Psiloscirtus splendidus (((Parasitalces sexnotata + Sitalces volxemi) (Arimacris trinitatis (Caruaruacris bivittatus + Salvadoracris nigritus))) (((Eusitalces vittatus + Liebermannacris dorsualis) (Monneacris fascipes (Robustusacris balzapambae (Xiphiola borellii (Eujivarus fusiformis + Roppacris griseipes))))) (((Omalotettix obliquus + Orthoscapheus coryaceus) (Abracris dilecta + Jodacris ferruginea)) (Teinophaus saussurei (Rhachicreagra nothra (Agesander ruficornis + Ixalotettix compactus))))))). Further data on biogeography and a distribution map are provided.

Keywords

grasshoppers; phylogeny; Neotropical region; taxonomy; distribution

Scale insects (Hemiptera: Sternorrhyncha: Coccoidea) of the Krakatau Islands including species from adjacent Java

Douglas J. Williams, Douglass R. Miller

Pages: 43-52

Abstract

[en_US] In this paper we provide a list of all species of scale insects that have been collected in the Krakatau Islands since the catastrophic eruption of 1883. Included in the list are species collected during the La Trobe University Expedition in 1984 from the islands and from the adjacent mainland in Java and also species collected in earlier expeditions, including those during 1919–1933, discussed by Dammerman (1948). In addition, we describe two new species of armoured scales, Aulacaspis thorntoni Williams & Miller and Greeniella calophylli Williams & Miller and provide a key to the species of Greeniella Cockerell.

Keywords

Krakatau; introduced species; Aulacaspis thorntoni sp. n., Greeniella calophylli sp. n., key to Greeniella

A description of a larva of Ancyrona diversa Pic, 1921 and its phylogenetic implications (Coleoptera: Trogossitidae)

Jiří Kolibáč, Artem A. Zaitsev

Pages: 53-62

Abstract

[en_US] A larva of Ancyrona diversa Pic, 1921 (Coleoptera: Trogossitidae) associated with adults is described in detail. Its morphological features are compared with known larvae of the tribe Lophocaterini and the subfamilies Trogossitinae and Peltinae. Phylogenetic and systematic implications are discussed. A resurrection of the subfamily name Lophocaterinae is suggested for the trogossitid tribes Decamerini, Lophocaterini and Ancyronini.

Keywords

Russian Far East; Coleoptera; Trogossitidae; Lophocaterini; Ancyronini; larval morphology; phylogeny

A taxonomic study of the genus Midoria Kato (Hemiptera: Cicadellidae: Ledrinae), with descriptions of two new species from China

Yujian Li, Zizhong Li

Pages: 63-68

Abstract

[en_US] The paper deals with eight species of the genus Midoria Kato, 1931 (Hemiptera: Cicadellidae: Ledrinae), including three new combinations and two new species, Midoria emmrichi (Zhang et al, 2004) comb. nov., Midoria longicornis (Zhang et al, 2004) comb. nov., Midoria funebris (Jacobi, 1944) comb. nov., Midoria huapingensis sp. nov. and Midoria lamellata sp. nov.. A key to distinguish all species of the genus (except Midoria capitata Kato) is given, and illustrations of genitalia are provided. The type specimens of new species are deposited in the Institute of Entomology, Guizhou University (GUGC).

Keywords

Homoptera; morphology; taxonomy; distribution; identification
